# US accounts after returning to China

Scenario: **PRC national** studies in the US (e.g. F1), opens bank + brokerage accounts, then **returns to mainland** while accounts remain open.

## What persists

| Asset | After return |
|-------|--------------|
| **SSN** | Permanent; does not expire |
| **US bank account** | Often keepable; update address/KYC |
| **US brokerage** | **Broker policy-dependent** |

## Can you trade US stocks from China?

**Technically often yes; compliance is multi-layered:**

1. **Broker rules** — Schwab International / IBKR more tolerant; Fidelity may restrict non-residents.
2. **China FX** — **New** RMB→USD for offshore trading subject to [[china-personal-fx-controls]].
3. **Tax** — PRC tax residents: **worldwide income** + **CRS** reporting.
4. **Sensitive IPOs** — Jurisdiction gates (e.g. SpaceX) apply regardless of SSN.

## SSN is not a global bypass

Does not override mainland capital-account restrictions on **new** outbound investment funds or ITAR-restricted IPO access.

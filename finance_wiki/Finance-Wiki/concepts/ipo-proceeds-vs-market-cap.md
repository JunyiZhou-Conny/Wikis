# IPO proceeds vs market cap

**IPO gross proceeds** and **market capitalization** answer different questions. Conflating them makes it look like there is a “gap” of trillions that someone must fill.

## Definitions

| Term | Formula (simplified) | Meaning |
|------|----------------------|---------|
| **IPO proceeds** | New shares sold × offer price | Cash entering the **issuer’s** treasury |
| **Market cap** | Total shares outstanding × price | Paper value of **all** equity at that price |

## SpaceX (SPCX) example — June 2026

- **Offer price:** $135 / share
- **Primary shares sold:** ~555.6M (+ overallotment → ~638.9M)
- **Gross proceeds:** ~$75–86B → **new money to SpaceX**
- **Post-IPO shares outstanding:** ~13.1B
- **Market cap:** ~$1.77T

The ~$1.7T “gap” vs proceeds is **not missing funding**. It is mostly **pre-existing shares** re-marked at the IPO price.

## Primary vs secondary

```text
IPO (primary):     Company ──new shares──► Investors     (cash → company)
After listing:     Investor A ◄──shares──► Investor B     (cash → seller, not company)
```
